Calculating an aura solely through averages can be unrepresentative because averages flatten the diversity of a listener's habits. A listener might have two distinct "moods"—say, high-energy gym music and low-energy study music—that average out to a "moderate" aura that they never actually experience. Furthermore, simple averages don't account for play count; a song played 500 times has the same weight as a song played 10 times if they are both in the top 100. A more representative approach would be to: 
1. Use weighted averages based on actual play counts (stream frequency).
2. Segment songs by time of day or day of week to find "temporal auras."
3. Use clustering algorithms (like K-Means) to identify the 2-3 most frequent mood clusters, rather than forced-averaging everything into one.
